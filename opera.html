The Mikado

CHORUS OF NOBLES-—Courtyard of Ko-Ko's Palace in Titipu. Japanese nobles discovered standing and sitting in attitudes suggested by native drawings

If you want to know who we are, We are gentlemen of Japan: On many a vase and jar— On many a screen and fan, We figure in lively paint: Our attitude's queer and quaint— You're wrong if you think it ain't, oh! If you think we are worked by strings, Like a Japanese marionette, You don't understand these things: It is simply Court etiquette. Perhaps you suppose this throng Can't keep it up all day long? If that's your idea, you're wrong, oh!

PISH

And what may be your business with Yum-Yum?

NANK

But how good of you

(for I see that you are a nobleman of the highest rank)

to condescend to tell all this to me, a mere strolling minstrel!

POOH

The fact appears to be as you've recited: But here he comes, equipped as suits his station; He'll give you any further information.

KO

Exactly—as the city will have to pay for it. That is your advice.

YUM

Well, of course I know nothing about these things; but I've no objection if it's usual.

PITTI

Oh, I expect it's all right. Must have a beginning, you know.

PEEP
(who has been examining Pooh-Bah).

I beg your pardon, but what is this? Customer come to try on?
